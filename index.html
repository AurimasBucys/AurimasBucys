My Process

Research before creative.
Always.

Most agencies jump straight to making ads. I build a full strategic foundation first so every creative decision has a reason behind it.

01
🎯
Customer Avatar
Deep profiling of the ideal buyer — demographics, frustrations, desires, objections, language they use online. Not assumptions. Research.
02
📊
Market Research
Competitor ads, winning angles, market sophistication level. I find what's already working before proposing anything new.
03
💡
Offer Brief
Positioning the product against the real alternative in the customer's mind. What makes it worth buying right now, for this person.
04
🔁
Belief-Shift Map
Mapping the mental journey from cold audience to buyer — what beliefs need to shift, and what creative angles address each one.

About Me

I treat ads as a
business problem.

I'm Aurimas, a performance marketer based in Vilnius. I got into this properly by running my own Shopify stores and managing Meta Ads with real money at stake. That experience taught me more than any course could about what actually makes people click and buy.

I went through the FastTrack LT digital marketing bootcamp to fill in the technical gaps and sharpen my paid media knowledge. Not long after, I got a take-home assignment from Memby/Digiklasė and used it as a chance to show what a structured research process looks like in practice.

The thing that drives most of my work is starting with the customer, not the creative. Before I open any tool, I want to know who I'm talking to, what they already believe, and what needs to shift. That approach shapes everything downstream and it's what makes the difference between an ad that looks good and one that actually works.
